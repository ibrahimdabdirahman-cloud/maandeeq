01 — Visiting

Hours, parking & getting here.

5 questions
01

What are your opening hours?

We are open Tuesday through Sunday, from 11:30 am to 10:30 pm. The kitchen runs late on Friday and Saturday evenings until 11:00 pm to look after the post-Maghrib crowd. We're closed on Mondays so our team can rest.

Shaah service continues for thirty minutes after the kitchen closes — never rushed.

HoursTue–Sun
02

Where are you, and how do I get there?

We are in the heart of Woolwich, South East London (SE18), a four-minute walk from Woolwich Arsenal station (Elizabeth Line, DLR, National Rail). The 53, 54, 96, 161, 177, 178, 244 and 380 buses all stop within two minutes of our door.

If you're coming by river, the Woolwich pier is a ten-minute walk through the market.

LocationElizabeth LineDLR
03

Is there parking nearby?

There's on-street pay-and-display on the surrounding streets — free after 6:30 pm and all day on Sunday. The closest paid car park is Love Lane Car Park, a three-minute walk away, with around 300 spaces.

We're inside the South Circular but outside the ULEZ congestion zone borders that get most people — but always double-check your route on TfL.

ParkingCar
04

Is the restaurant step-free and accessible?

Yes. We have step-free entry from the street, wide aisles between tables, and an accessible WC on the ground floor. If you'd like a table close to the entrance or away from the speakers during a busy service, just let us know when you book and we'll save the right spot for you.

AccessibilityStep-free
05

Is there a prayer space on site?

We have a small, quiet prayer area with mats and a copy of the qibla direction. Woolwich Central Mosque is also a four-minute walk away if you'd prefer to pray in jama'ah. We're happy to time your courses around prayer — just give us a quiet nod and we'll hold the food.

SalahPrayer space
02 — Halal & Sourcing

A kitchen that's halal end-to-end.

4 questions
06

Is everything 100% halal?

Yes — completely. Every protein on our menu is HMC- or HFA-certified halal, sourced from a small set of trusted London-area butchers. We do not serve alcohol, pork, or any haram ingredient. There is no separate "non-halal" section to worry about.

If you ever want to see a certificate, ask any member of staff and we'll bring it to your table.

HalalHMCHFA
07

Is the meat hand-slaughtered or stunned?

Our lamb and chicken are HMC-certified, which means hand-slaughtered, non-stunned, with the tasmiyah recited at each animal. Our beef is HFA-certified. We keep both certificates on file and refresh them annually.

HMCNon-stunned
08

Do you serve alcohol?

No. We are an alcohol-free restaurant by choice, and guests are kindly asked not to bring their own. Instead we pour generous spiced shaah, fresh juices, mango lassi, and a short list of soft drinks. Many guests tell us the food tastes better for it.

Alcohol-freeBYOB
09

Where do you source your meat and produce?

Lamb comes daily from a long-standing halal butcher in Edmonton; chicken from a family-run supplier in Leyton. Produce is picked up most mornings at New Spitalfields Market. Our cardamom, cumin and coriander are sourced through an East African importer in Whitechapel — and ground in our kitchen each morning.

SourcingSpices
03 — Menu & Dietary

What's on the plate, and what's not.

6 questions
10

What's your signature dish?

The Maandeeq Special Haniid Lamb Shank — slow-roasted for eight hours with our house xawaash, served on a bed of fragrant bariis rice with caramelised onions. If you're ordering for the first time, this is the one. If you only ever order one thing from us, let it be this.

SignatureHaniid
11

Do you have vegetarian or vegan options?

Yes, more than people expect. Our vegetable suqaar, bariis with grilled halloumi, foul medames, spiced lentils, and most of our breakfast spread are vegetarian. Vegan-by-default options are marked with a small leaf on the menu; ask our team and they'll walk you through what can be adapted (most things can).

VegetarianVegan
12

Can you handle allergies?

Please tell your server before you order. We carry a full allergen book and a kitchen team trained to handle gluten, dairy, nut, sesame and shellfish allergies. We do prepare nuts and sesame in our kitchen, so we cannot guarantee an allergen-free environment — but we'll be honest with you about what is and isn't safe on any given day.

AllergiesAllergens
13

Is your food very spicy?

Somali food is aromatic, not fiery. Our xawaash is built on cumin, coriander, cardamom and cinnamon — warmth without heat. If you'd like a kick, ask for a side of basbaas — our house green chilli sauce — and add it to taste.

SpiceBasbaas
14

Do you serve breakfast?

Yes — and breakfast is one of our favourite services. From 11:30 am until 1:00 pm we serve malawax with honey, anjero with suqaar, foul medames, and eggs to order. Best paired with shaah and a slow morning.

BreakfastMalawax
15

What's the difference between haniid, suqaar & chipsi maya?

Haniid is slow-roasted meat — typically lamb shank — cooked low and long until it falls off the bone. Suqaar is cubed meat or vegetables, sautéed quickly with onion, garlic and xawaash. Chipsi maya is a Tanzanian-Swahili chip omelette — comfort food we serve in honour of East Africa's shared kitchen.

You'll find a full glossary on our About page.

04 — Bookings & Events

Reserving, catering, celebrating.

5 questions
16

Do I need a reservation?

Walk-ins are always welcome — there's almost always a table at lunchtime. For Friday and Saturday evenings, and for any party of five or more, we'd recommend booking ahead. You can reserve online or call us.

Walk-inBooking
17

Can you host large groups or private events?

Yes. Our back room seats up to 38 guests and can be booked privately on quieter evenings. For larger gatherings — Nikkah parties, walimah, baby showers, Eid lunches — we can do full-venue hires for up to 90 guests. Email events@maandeeq.co.uk with your date and headcount and we'll send you our event sheet.

Private hireEvents
18

Do you offer catering for off-site events?

We cater across Greater London. Our most popular catering format is the family platter — slow-roasted lamb on a sea of bariis, with sides for ten to fifty guests. Mega Fusion Platters are also available. We ask for seven days' notice, more for parties over 100.

CateringPlatters
19

What's your cancellation policy?

For tables of up to eight, cancellation is free up until two hours before your booking. For larger parties and private hire we hold a small refundable deposit per head, returned in full if you cancel 72 hours in advance. Plans change — we understand. Just let us know as soon as you can.

CancellationDeposit
20

Can you do Eid and Ramadan sittings?

Yes — and they are the busiest, brightest weeks of our year. During Ramadan we run a communal iftar sitting nightly with dates, sambusas and shaah at the table when you arrive, timed precisely to the call. For Eid we operate a set-menu service with sittings every two hours. Bookings open in early Sha'ban — join our list to be told first.

IftarEidRamadan
05 — Delivery & Takeaway

From our kitchen to your door.

4 questions
21

Do you do delivery?

Yes. We deliver via Deliveroo, Uber Eats and Just Eat within a four-mile radius of Woolwich. For platters, large orders and same-day catering it's faster (and kinder on you) to call the restaurant directly and we'll arrange a courier.

DeliveryDeliveroo
22

Can I collect a takeaway?

Of course. Call ahead with your order and we'll have it boxed at the front counter — usually 15 to 25 minutes from order, longer for slow-roasted dishes which we recommend pre-ordering for the day before. There's no service fee on collection.

TakeawayCollection
23

How do I reheat what I've taken home?

For the lamb and chicken: cover loosely with foil and warm in the oven at 160°C for 12–15 minutes. Splash a spoon of water on the bariis to revive it in a covered pan over low heat. Microwaves work; ovens reward you.

ReheatingTips
24

Is the packaging recyclable?

Our takeaway boxes are bagasse (sugarcane fibre), fully compostable. Lids and cutlery are PLA-based bioplastic. Carrier bags are kraft paper. Please pop them in your food waste bin where you can.

PackagingCompostable

Are children welcome?

Always. Maandeeq is a family kitchen and children make the room feel right. We have highchairs, a small children's menu (chicken suqaar, mild bariis, sabayaad strips), and the kitchen is happy to plate things "the way your child eats at home" if you tell our team how.

Is there a women-only section?

We don't operate a permanent partition, but our back room can be reserved as a women-only or family-only space for private parties — Nikkah after-parties, baby showers, ladies' lunches. Mention it when you book.

Private hireFamily room

Do you take tips? Is service included?

An optional 10% service charge is added to tables of six or more — it goes directly and fully to the team that served you, distributed weekly. For smaller tables, tipping is appreciated but never expected. A word of thanks to the aunties carries equal weight in our room.

ServiceTipping
