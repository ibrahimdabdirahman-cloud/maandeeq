01 — Our story

Tradition on every plate.

A family recipe, served openly

Maandeeq was born out of a deep love for community and a passion for generational family recipes. In Somali culture, food is never just a meal — it is a celebration of togetherness, generosity, and heritage. We wanted to create a space in London that captured exactly that feeling.

A welcoming, comfortable home where friends and families can gather over generous platters of incredible food. The kind of room where the conversation lasts longer than the meal, and the second pot of shaah is always already on.

We pride ourselves on doing things the traditional way. That means no shortcuts in our kitchen: from blending our own aromatic xawaash spices each morning, to slow-roasting our meats until they fall straight off the bone. Every dish is a labour of love, crafted to honour the culinary traditions of our ancestors.

03 — More than a restaurant

A community hub.

The Somali spirit of generosity — Soo Dhaweyn — is woven into everything we do. When you walk through our doors, you aren't a customer. You are our guest.

At Maandeeq, our hospitality is as famous as our lamb shanks. Our spacious, family-friendly setting is designed for comfort — perfect for everything from a quick solo lunch to grand family celebrations and private events.

The aunties in our kitchen and our friendly front-of-house staff are here to ensure your dining experience is flawless from the moment you sit down to the last sip of your spiced Somali tea.

A few words from our kitchen

The Somali pantry

Some terms you'll meet on our menu — and what they mean at our table.

Xawaash

The foundation spice blend of every Somali kitchen — cumin, coriander, cardamom, cinnamon and more, ground fresh. Our morning ritual.

Haniid

Slow-roasted lamb, traditionally cooked low and long until the meat surrenders. Our signature.

Bariis

Fragrant Somali basmati rice, perfumed with whole spices and caramelised onion. The bed for everything.

Sabayaad

A buttery, flaky flatbread cooked on a hot griddle. For tearing, scooping, sharing.

Malawax

A soft, lacy pancake-bread — sweet or savoury, served morning or night. Honey is non-negotiable.

Shaah

Spiced black tea with cardamom, clove, cinnamon and milk. The end of every meal, the start of every conversation.

Soo Dhaweyn

The Somali welcome — generosity without expectation. The reason we cook.

Chipsi Maya

A Tanzanian-influenced chip omelette — comfort food we serve in the Swahili tradition. East Africa, on a single plate.
